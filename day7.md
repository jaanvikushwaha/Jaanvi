Day 7 - Claude Usage Strategy

🎯 Strategy

Based on my profile as a Computer Science student, the recommended primary Claude model is Sonnet. It offers the best balance of speed, reasoning, and quality for daily academic work, coding, resume improvement, and learning.

---

🤖 Model Usage

- Haiku: Quick lookups, simple questions, and short summaries.
- Sonnet: Daily coding, assignments, resume optimization, debugging, and project development.
- Opus: High-stakes tasks such as final resumes, capstone projects, research work, and job applications.

---

⚡ Effort Strategy

- Low Effort: Basic queries and quick answers.
- Standard Effort (55%): Daily learning, coding, and assignments.
- High Effort (30%): Debugging, resume optimization, and complex problem-solving.
- Max Effort: Final resume review, major projects, and job application preparation.

🔑 Key Learnings

- Sonnet is the best default model for most daily tasks.
- Use Standard effort for the majority of work.
- Switch to High effort for debugging and resume optimization.
- Reserve Opus with Max effort for important career-related tasks.
- Choosing the right model and effort level improves productivity and response quality.

🚀 Outcome

I learned how to select the appropriate Claude model and effort level based on different tasks, helping me work more efficiently and effectively.
